Washington DC

Washington, D.C. is the capital city and federal district of the United States. It is located on the east bank of the Potomac River. The city was named for George Washington, a Founding Father and the first president of the United States. As the seat of the U.S. federal government and several international organizations, the city is an important world political capital. It is one of the most visited cities in the U.S., seeing over 20 million visitors in 2016! Washington has incredible museums and monuments, the streets are very clean, which is always a plus...

The weather in DC

Summers are hot and humid with a July daily average of 79.8 °F (26.6 °C) and average daily relative humidity around 66%. Winters are cold and sometimes it even snows.

** July is the hottest month in Washington, the hottest hour is 6:00 pm!

Transportation in DC

Washington DC public transportation is great. The Meto is an affordable and convenient way to get around the DC metropolitan area.

Architecture in DC

The definitive architectural style in DC, and especially in the "Mall" is neoclassical, inspired by the use of ancient Greek and Roman designs of great public buildings. Did you know? The height of a building in DC is permitted to be max 90 ft so it won't be higher than the Washington Monument!
